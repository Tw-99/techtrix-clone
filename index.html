How it works

ChainGrade

Immutable academic records. AI-screened, faculty-signed, blockchain-anchored.

Submit your project once and receive a permanent on-chain record. Employers, institutions, and reviewers can verify it instantly through cryptographic proof: no university call required, no waiting period.

The problem

Credentials expire. Records shouldn't.

Academic records are only as reliable as the institution holding them. If a university closes, loses accreditation, or simply does not respond to a verification request, your credential becomes worthless.

ChainGrade writes each submission to a public blockchain. The record is cryptographically tied to the submitter's wallet, not to any issuing institution. It persists permanently, and any party with the content hash can verify its authenticity independently.

Three steps

01

Connect your wallet

Your wallet address becomes your permanent identity on the network. No email, no password required; only the cryptographic key you already control.

→
02

Upload your project

Your file is stored on IPFS, a distributed storage network, and a unique cryptographic fingerprint is written on-chain. Alter even one character and the fingerprint no longer matches.

→
03

Faculty verify it

Faculty reviewers each sign off with their own wallet. Every signature is recorded on-chain. Anyone can audit who approved, when they approved, and exactly what they signed.

→
⬖

Ready? Connect your wallet to begin.

ChainGrade runs on Polygon Amoy (testnet). You'll need MetaMask or any compatible wallet extension. Your public address becomes your identity — no sign-up required.

ChainGrade never stores your private key. All signatures are produced locally by your wallet. Only your public address and signed assertions are submitted to the ledger.
